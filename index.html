S3 Storage Classes

S3 Standard — $0.023/GB/month
Default storage class. 99.99% availability, 11 nines durability, multi-AZ. No minimum storage duration. Best for frequently accessed data, web applications, and content distribution. Retrieval is free — you only pay for storage and requests.

S3 Intelligent-Tiering — $0.023/GB (frequent) / $0.0125/GB (infrequent)
Automatically moves objects between frequent and infrequent access tiers based on usage patterns. Monitoring fee: $0.0025 per 1,000 objects. No retrieval fees. Best for data with unknown or changing access patterns. Objects not accessed for 30 days move to infrequent tier. Deep Archive tier available for objects not accessed 180+ days.

S3 Standard-IA (Infrequent Access) — $0.0125/GB/month
46% cheaper than Standard for storage, but charges $0.01/GB retrieval fee. 30-day minimum storage duration. Best for backups, disaster recovery files, and data accessed monthly or less. Per-GB retrieval cost makes it expensive for frequently read data.

S3 One Zone-IA — $0.01/GB/month
Like Standard-IA but stored in a single AZ — 20% cheaper than Standard-IA but less durable (99.5% availability). Best for reproducible secondary copies, thumbnails, and data that can be re-created if the AZ fails. Not suitable for primary backup storage.

FAQ — AWS S3 Pricing
S3 Standard: ~$0.023/GB/month. First 50TB discounted; above 500TB at $0.021/GB. Plus request costs: PUT/COPY $0.005 per 1,000 requests, GET $0.0004 per 1,000. Data transfer out first 100GB/month free, then $0.09/GB up to 10TB. Most small projects pay under $5/month. The AWS Free Tier includes 5GB Standard, 20,000 GET requests, and 2,000 PUT requests per month for 12 months.
New AWS accounts get 12 months free: 5 GB of S3 Standard storage, 20,000 GET requests, 2,000 PUT/COPY/POST/LIST requests, and 15 GB of data transfer out per month. After 12 months, standard rates apply. Some services like AWS Lambda have a permanent free tier, but S3 storage free tier expires after 12 months.
Intelligent-Tiering automatically moves objects between access tiers based on usage. Objects not accessed for 30 days move to Infrequent tier (50% cheaper). Objects not accessed for 90 days can move to Archive Instant tier. No retrieval fees. Monitoring fee: $0.0025 per 1,000 objects. Best for large datasets with unpredictable access. Not cost-effective for objects under 128KB or data accessed frequently.
Key optimizations: (1) Use lifecycle policies to automatically move infrequently accessed objects to Standard-IA or Glacier. (2) Enable Intelligent-Tiering for large buckets with unpredictable access. (3) Use CloudFront in front of S3 — transfer from S3 to CloudFront is free. (4) Delete incomplete multipart uploads (hidden cost). (5) Compress objects before storing to reduce GB. (6) Use S3 Storage Lens to identify unused buckets and large objects.
PUT, COPY, POST, LIST: $0.005 per 1,000 requests. GET, SELECT, and all other requests: $0.0004 per 1,000 requests. Lifecycle transition requests: $0.01 per 1,000. For Standard-IA and One Zone-IA, GET requests also include a $0.01/GB retrieval fee. High-volume applications with millions of requests pay significant request costs — use caching to minimize GET requests.
